## Results

- The differentiable **Asterion-SAM** reproduces the $z = 0$ stellar-to-halo mass relation only when fitted jointly to cold-gas fractions and gas-phase metallicities, reducing the median residual from 0.31 dex to 0.08 dex.[^1]
- Exact Jacobians show that the wind energy-loading amplitude $A_E$ dominates sensitivity across $10^{10.5}$-$10^{12.5}\,\mathrm{M}_\odot$ haloes, while mass loading $\eta_M$ mainly changes low-mass cold-gas reservoirs.[^2]
- The posterior favours low mass loading, $\eta_M \lesssim 1$ for a Milky Way-mass halo, but high **energy loading**, $\eta_E \approx 0.6$ for dwarf haloes, indicating preventative CGM heating rather than direct ISM ejection.[^3]
